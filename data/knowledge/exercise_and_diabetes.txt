Exercise and Diabetes Management

Physical activity is a cornerstone of diabetes management. Regular exercise improves insulin sensitivity, helps control blood glucose, and reduces cardiovascular risk.

ADA RECOMMENDATIONS:
- 150+ minutes per week of moderate-to-vigorous aerobic activity
- Resistance exercise 2-3 times per week
- No more than 2 consecutive days without aerobic exercise
- Break up prolonged sitting every 30 minutes

EFFECTS ON BLOOD GLUCOSE:
- Aerobic exercise generally LOWERS blood glucose (muscles use glucose for energy)
- High-intensity/anaerobic exercise can temporarily RAISE blood glucose (stress hormones)
- Effects can last 24-48 hours post-exercise

BLOOD GLUCOSE MANAGEMENT AROUND EXERCISE:
- Check blood glucose before, during (if >30 min), and after exercise
- Safe range to exercise: 100-250 mg/dL (consult your doctor for your targets)
- Below 100 mg/dL: eat 15-30g carbs before exercise
- Above 250 mg/dL with ketones: postpone exercise
- Above 300 mg/dL: use caution, postpone until glucose is controlled

HYPOGLYCEMIA DURING EXERCISE:
- More common with type 1 diabetes
- Carry fast-acting carbohydrates (glucose tablets, juice)
- May need to reduce insulin dose before exercise (consult provider)
- Post-exercise hypoglycemia can occur hours later — monitor overnight

BEST TYPES OF EXERCISE:
- Walking (most accessible, consistently beneficial)
- Swimming, cycling (low impact, good for joint issues)
- Strength training (increases muscle mass, improves insulin sensitivity)
- Yoga (also reduces stress, which affects blood sugar)

Always consult your healthcare provider before starting a new exercise program.

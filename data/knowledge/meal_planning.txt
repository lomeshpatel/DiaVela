Diabetes-Friendly Meal Planning

Healthy eating is fundamental to diabetes management. There is no single "diabetes diet" — but evidence-based patterns can help manage blood glucose.

EVIDENCE-BASED EATING PATTERNS FOR DIABETES:
1. Mediterranean Diet: rich in vegetables, whole grains, legumes, fish, olive oil; moderate dairy, eggs, poultry; limited red meat and sweets. Reduces cardiovascular risk.
2. DASH Diet: designed to lower blood pressure; high in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, low-fat dairy; limits saturated fat, sodium, sweets.
3. Low-Carbohydrate Diet: <130g carbs/day or <26% of calories. Effective for glucose control and weight loss. Requires medication adjustment.
4. Very Low-Carbohydrate/Ketogenic: <50g carbs/day. Very effective short-term; requires close medical supervision.
5. Plant-Based/Vegan: associated with improved glucose control, weight, and cardiovascular health.

THE DIABETES PLATE METHOD (simple approach):
- Fill 1/2 plate with non-starchy vegetables (salad, broccoli, carrots, peppers)
- Fill 1/4 plate with lean protein (chicken, fish, tofu, eggs, beans)
- Fill 1/4 plate with carbohydrate foods (whole grains, starchy vegetables, legumes, fruit)
- Add a small amount of healthy fat
- Drink water or a low-calorie beverage

FOODS TO EMPHASIZE:
- Non-starchy vegetables (unlimited): all leafy greens, cucumber, tomato, pepper, mushrooms, zucchini
- Lean proteins: fish (especially fatty fish twice/week), chicken breast, turkey, eggs, tofu
- Healthy fats: avocado, nuts, seeds, olive oil
- High-fiber whole grains: oatmeal, quinoa, whole wheat, barley
- Legumes: beans, lentils, chickpeas (high fiber, moderate carb)
- Low-glycemic fruits: berries, apple, pear, citrus

FOODS TO LIMIT:
- Sugary beverages (regular soda, juice, energy drinks, sweet tea)
- Refined carbs and white starches (white bread, white rice, crackers)
- Processed/ultra-processed foods
- Saturated and trans fats (fried foods, butter, full-fat dairy, processed meats)
- High-sodium foods (raises blood pressure)
- Alcohol (can cause hypoglycemia, especially if on insulin or sulfonylureas)

PRACTICAL TIPS:
- Eat at consistent times each day
- Don't skip meals (especially if on insulin/sulfonylureas)
- Read nutrition labels: look at total carbohydrates, not just sugar
- Eat slowly — it takes 20 minutes to feel full
- Choose whole foods over processed alternatives

Work with a registered dietitian (RD) or certified diabetes care and education specialist (CDCES) for a personalized meal plan.

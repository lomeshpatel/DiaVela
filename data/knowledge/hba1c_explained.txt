HbA1c (Hemoglobin A1c) Explained

HbA1c measures your average blood glucose over the past 2-3 months. It reflects the percentage of hemoglobin proteins in your blood that have glucose attached.

HbA1c ranges:
- Normal (no diabetes): Below 5.7%
- Prediabetes: 5.7% to 6.4%
- Diabetes diagnosis: 6.5% or higher
- Well-controlled diabetes target: Below 7% (for most adults)
- Higher target (less stringent): 7-8% (for elderly, limited life expectancy, or frequent hypoglycemia)

Approximate average blood glucose by HbA1c:
- 6%   → ~126 mg/dL
- 7%   → ~154 mg/dL
- 8%   → ~183 mg/dL
- 9%   → ~212 mg/dL
- 10%  → ~240 mg/dL
- 11%  → ~269 mg/dL
- 12%  → ~298 mg/dL

HbA1c is typically measured every 3 months in people not meeting treatment goals, and every 6 months in those who are stable and meeting goals.

Limitations: HbA1c may not accurately reflect glucose control in people with certain conditions (sickle cell disease, hemolytic anemia, recent blood transfusion, iron deficiency anemia).

Always consult your healthcare provider for interpretation of your HbA1c results.

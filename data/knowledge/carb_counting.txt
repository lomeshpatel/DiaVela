Carbohydrate Counting for Diabetes Management

Carbohydrate counting (carb counting) is a meal planning tool that helps people with diabetes manage blood glucose levels. Carbohydrates have the most direct effect on blood sugar.

What counts as a carbohydrate serving?
- 1 serving = 15 grams of carbohydrates
- Most people with diabetes aim for 45-60 grams of carbs per meal (3-4 servings)
- Snacks: 15-30 grams (1-2 servings)

High-carb foods to monitor:
- Grains: bread (1 slice = ~15g), rice (1/3 cup cooked = 15g), pasta (1/3 cup cooked = 15g)
- Starchy vegetables: potato (1/2 cup = ~15g), corn (1/2 cup = ~15g)
- Fruits: apple (1 small = ~15g), banana (1/2 medium = ~15g), grapes (17 small = ~15g)
- Dairy: milk (1 cup = ~12g), yogurt (varies, check label)
- Legumes: beans (1/3 cup = ~15g), lentils (1/4 cup = ~15g)
- Sweets: cookies, cake, candy, sweetened beverages

Low-carb foods (less than 5g per serving, usually free foods):
- Non-starchy vegetables: broccoli, lettuce, cucumber, peppers, cauliflower, spinach
- Meat, poultry, fish, eggs (no carbs)
- Fats: oils, butter, nuts (small amounts)
- Most cheeses

Glycemic Index (GI): Foods with high GI raise blood sugar faster. Prefer low-GI options like whole grains, legumes, and non-starchy vegetables.

The glycemic load (GL) considers both GI and the amount of carbohydrate in a portion.

Always work with a registered dietitian to create a personalized meal plan.

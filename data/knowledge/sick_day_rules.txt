Sick Day Rules for People with Diabetes

Illness, infection, and stress can raise blood glucose significantly due to stress hormones (cortisol, glucagon). Follow these guidelines during illness.

GENERAL SICK DAY RULES:
1. Never stop taking insulin (even if you're not eating) — you may need MORE
2. Monitor blood glucose every 2-4 hours
3. Check urine or blood ketones if glucose >240 mg/dL (type 1) or >300 mg/dL (type 2)
4. Stay hydrated: aim for 8+ oz fluid per hour
5. Contact your healthcare provider early

WHEN TO CALL YOUR DOCTOR:
- Blood glucose above 240 mg/dL for more than 2 checks
- Moderate or large ketones in urine
- Unable to keep fluids down for >4 hours (vomiting/diarrhea)
- Illness lasting more than 2 days without improvement
- Signs of dehydration (extreme thirst, dry mouth, dark urine)
- Confusion or difficulty breathing

STAYING HYDRATED WHEN SICK:
- If blood glucose is low or normal: drink regular juice, sports drinks, or regular soda
- If blood glucose is high: drink water, broth, diet drinks (no sugar)
- Try to eat 15g carbs every hour if you can't eat regular meals

MEDICATIONS TO AVOID WHEN SICK:
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) — can affect kidneys; use acetaminophen instead
- Decongestants can raise blood glucose
- SGLT2 inhibitors — increased DKA risk when sick/not eating; consult provider

KETOACIDOSIS WARNING SIGNS (Call 911 or go to ER):
- Fruity-smelling breath
- Nausea/vomiting
- Abdominal pain
- Rapid or labored breathing
- Confusion or extreme fatigue

Keep a sick day kit: glucose tablets, ketone strips, clear fluids, thermometer, and your healthcare provider's contact information.

Blood Glucose Monitoring and Continuous Glucose Monitors (CGM)

SELF-MONITORING OF BLOOD GLUCOSE (SMBG):
Using a fingerstick glucometer to check blood glucose levels.

When to check (varies by treatment plan):
- Before meals (fasting)
- 2 hours after meals (postprandial)
- Before bedtime
- Before, during, and after exercise
- When feeling symptoms of low or high blood sugar
- Before driving
- At 2-3 AM if nocturnal hypoglycemia is suspected

Glucometer accuracy: should be within 15% or 15 mg/dL of laboratory values. Check calibration, expiration dates, and proper strip storage.

CONTINUOUS GLUCOSE MONITORS (CGM):
CGMs use a small sensor inserted under the skin to measure glucose in interstitial fluid every 1-5 minutes.

MAJOR CGM SYSTEMS:
- Dexcom G7/G6: 10-day wear, real-time readings, integrates with insulin pumps
- FreeStyle Libre 3 (Abbott): 14-day wear, real-time Bluetooth readings, smallest sensor
- Medtronic Guardian: integrates with Medtronic insulin pumps
- Eversense E3: implantable sensor, 365-day wear (US), physician-inserted

CGM METRICS (Time in Range - TIR):
- Target range for most adults: 70-180 mg/dL
- Goal TIR: >70% of the time
- Time below range (<70 mg/dL): <4% (goal <1% below 54 mg/dL)
- Time above range (>180 mg/dL): <25%

CGM BENEFITS:
- Continuous trend data (not just snapshots)
- Alerts for high/low glucose
- Reduces hypoglycemia risk and anxiety
- Better HbA1c achievement
- Identifies patterns (dawn phenomenon, post-meal spikes)

CGM LIMITATIONS:
- Lag time of 5-15 minutes vs. blood glucose
- May require fingerstick confirmation for treatment decisions (older models)
- Sensor can be inaccurate if placed incorrectly or during compression (sleeping on sensor)
- Cost (insurance coverage varies)

Consult your endocrinologist or diabetes care team about which monitoring approach is right for you.

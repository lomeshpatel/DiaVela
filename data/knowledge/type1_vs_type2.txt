Type 1 vs Type 2 Diabetes

TYPE 1 DIABETES:
- Autoimmune condition: the immune system destroys insulin-producing beta cells in the pancreas
- The body produces little or no insulin
- Onset: typically childhood or young adulthood, but can occur at any age
- Treatment: requires insulin therapy (multiple daily injections or insulin pump)
- About 5-10% of all diabetes cases
- Not preventable; not caused by diet or lifestyle
- Symptoms appear quickly: extreme thirst, frequent urination, weight loss, fatigue, blurred vision

TYPE 2 DIABETES:
- The body doesn't use insulin effectively (insulin resistance) and can't make enough insulin
- Most common form: ~90-95% of all diabetes cases
- Strong association with overweight/obesity, physical inactivity, family history
- Onset: typically adults 45+, but increasingly in younger people
- Treatment: lifestyle changes, oral medications (e.g., metformin), and sometimes insulin
- Can often be prevented or delayed with healthy lifestyle
- Symptoms develop gradually; some people have no symptoms initially

GESTATIONAL DIABETES:
- Develops during pregnancy
- Usually resolves after delivery
- Increases risk of type 2 diabetes later in life

PREDIABETES:
- Blood sugar higher than normal but not yet type 2 diabetes (HbA1c 5.7-6.4%)
- Affects ~96 million American adults
- Can often be reversed with lifestyle changes (5-7% weight loss, 150 min/week exercise)

Consult your healthcare provider for diagnosis and personalized treatment.

Common Diabetes Medications

NON-INSULIN MEDICATIONS FOR TYPE 2 DIABETES:

METFORMIN (Glucophage):
- First-line medication for type 2 diabetes
- Mechanism: reduces glucose production in the liver, improves insulin sensitivity
- Benefits: weight neutral/modest weight loss, low hypoglycemia risk, cardioprotective, affordable
- Side effects: GI upset (nausea, diarrhea) — take with food; rare risk of lactic acidosis
- Take with meals

SGLT2 INHIBITORS (Empagliflozin/Jardiance, Dapagliflozin/Farxiga, Canagliflozin/Invokana):
- Mechanism: cause kidneys to excrete excess glucose in urine
- Benefits: cardiovascular and kidney protection, weight loss, blood pressure reduction
- Side effects: genital yeast infections, UTIs, increased urination, rare DKA risk
- Take in the morning

GLP-1 RECEPTOR AGONISTS (Semaglutide/Ozempic/Wegovy, Liraglutide/Victoza, Dulaglutide/Trulicity):
- Mechanism: mimic GLP-1 hormone — stimulate insulin, suppress glucagon, slow gastric emptying
- Benefits: significant weight loss, cardiovascular protection, once weekly dosing available
- Side effects: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea (usually improves), rare pancreatitis
- Injectable (semaglutide also available as oral pill Rybelsus)

DPP-4 INHIBITORS (Sitagliptin/Januvia, Saxagliptin/Onglyza, Alogliptin/Nesina):
- Mechanism: increase incretin levels, stimulate insulin release
- Benefits: weight neutral, low hypoglycemia risk, well tolerated
- Side effects: nasopharyngitis, joint pain, rare pancreatitis

SULFONYLUREAS (Glipizide, Glimepiride, Glyburide):
- Mechanism: stimulate pancreas to produce more insulin
- Benefits: inexpensive, effective, long track record
- Side effects: hypoglycemia (significant risk), weight gain

THIAZOLIDINEDIONES (Pioglitazone/Actos):
- Mechanism: improve insulin sensitivity in fat and muscle
- Benefits: durable effect, may protect pancreatic beta cells
- Side effects: weight gain, fluid retention, increased fracture risk, bladder cancer risk (pioglitazone)

Always take medications as prescribed. Never stop or change doses without consulting your healthcare provider.
